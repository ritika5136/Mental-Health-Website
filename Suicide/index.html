Overview

Suicide

Suicide, taking your own life, is a tragic reaction to stressful life situations — and all the more tragic because suicide can be prevented. Whether you're considering suicide or know someone who feels suicidal, learn suicide warning signs and how to reach out for immediate help and professional treatment. You may save a life — your own or someone else's.
It may seem like there's no way to solve your problems and that suicide is the only way to end the pain.
Suicide attempts and death rates vary by sex, age, and race.
While suicide deaths are uncommon, suicide attempts and ideation (thought of killing oneself or wishing oneself dead) are less rare.

Inside a Suicidal Mind

Most people who kill themselves believe that suicide is the only solution to their unbearable situation. It is important that you show empathy to people suffering from depression and give them a listening ear. Most people make the biggest mistake of saying, ‘Be strong!’, ‘You cannot act so weak,’ which can work against them because their head is telling them exactly the opposite of what you are saying.” Sometimes suicidal thoughts or a persistent feeling of depression can be due to a chemical imbalance and they don’t have any control over it. And by telling them that they are being weak, you may be pushing them to the edge. Sometimes even a last minute call from a friend can make them change their mind. The demons in their heads are telling them it’s worthless to live, so listen to them and try to understand their concern. If you feel the issue is beyond your control, fix an appointment with a therapist and get them help!

The stigma surrounding mental illness arises from living in a culture where feelings of vulnerability are considered weak and unacceptable.
This is especially true for men who are raised with the injunction that “boys don’t cry”-i.e., it is not okay for men to be vulnerable and show their feelings.
This fear of being seen (by themselves and others) as vulnerable and weak, leads many men to lose touch with their own feelings and to avoid being in
situations where strong emotion may be present.
